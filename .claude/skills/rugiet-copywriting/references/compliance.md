## 1. Required disclaimers

Include the following disclaimers as appropriate.

### For creative with customer testimonials
Include the following in the same frame:

> "Customer's results have not been independently verified. Individual results may vary."

### For materials with paid actors
Include "Actor portrayal" or "Actor portrayals" as soon as the actor(s) appear on screen, for a minimum of **5 seconds**.

### For materials with onset time or effectiveness claims
Substantiate the claim and include "Individual results may vary."

### For the 15-minute onset claim on Rugiet Ready
Write:

> "*on average, after medication dissolves. Based on an internal survey of Rugiet Ready customers. Customer results have not been independently verified. Individual results may vary."

### For compounded medications
Include:

> "Compounded drugs may be prescribed under federal law but are not FDA-approved and do not undergo FDA safety, effectiveness, or manufacturing review."

## 2. Content compliance rules

- **No guarantees.** Avoid "guaranteed" and "miracle." Only use "works" when substantiated with internal or clinical data.
- **Use conditional claims.** Use terms like *help*, *designed to*, *can*, *could be* when direct evidence is not available to support a claim.
- **Avoid stating direct personal attributes.** Don't directly say "you have ED" in paid ads.
- **Avoid the word "sex" in ad captions, copy, and video hooks.** Using "sex" often leads to disapprovals on advertising platforms.
- **Avoid intimate bedroom scenes or suggestive imagery.** Often disapproved on ad platforms.
- **Disclose incentives.** State if reviewers receive discounts or free product.

## 4. Messaging playbook: Rugiet Ready claims cheat-sheet

**Overall tip:** Remove claims implying certainty or numeric superiority unless backed by internal studies or published data.

| Claim area | Do ✅ | Why? | Don't ❌ | Why not? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Speed of onset** | "Works in 15 minutes on average*," "Works faster" | Conditional, user-reported; aligns with data from studies | "Works in 15 minutes or less" | Absolute claim, unsubstantiated |
| **Apomorphine's mechanism** | "Helps amplify arousal" / "primes the brain for arousal" | Reflects apomorphine's mechanism of action (source 1)(source 2) | "Sparks arousal," "boosts desire," "ignites libido" | Implies apomorphine increases sexual desire, which is not shown (source) |
| **Comparative speed/strength** | Qualitative (e.g., "faster than traditional medications," "stronger formulation") | Avoids numeric superiority | "5× faster," "3× stronger," "strongest ED medication," "better than Cialis" | Absolute claims; numerical claims require head-to-head trial data |
| **Competitor references** | "Contains sildenafil," "contains tadalafil" | Informational; allowed nominative use of trademark | "Generic Viagra," "Better than Hims" | Generics are also FDA-approved; do not want to disparage competitors with compounded products |
| **FDA status and advertising regulations** | Add full compounded disclaimer (see §1) | Mandatory transparency | "FDA-approved," "meets FDA standards" | False for compounded products, even though individual ingredients are FDA-approved |
| **General efficacy** | "Can help," "could be the answer," "designed to," "formulated to" | Keeps claims conditional | "Fixes ED," "will solve your ED," "Guaranteed results" | Implies certainty |

## 5. Core compliance principles

### Truthful and non-misleading
- For substantiation of claims: link clinical studies, cite surveys, and keep audits for superlative claims on file.
- Avoid unqualified superlatives: don't use "only" or "first ___ medication" — implies approval and recognition from regulatory bodies.

### Conditional language
- ✅ **Do:** "Works in 15 minutes on average*," "Designed to work faster than traditional ED meds."
- ❌ **Don't:** "Works in less than 15 minutes." (claims below a certain time for all users)

### Compounded product and variability disclaimers
- Add the disclaimer for compounded medications (see §1).
- Include an audible or visible "Individual results may vary" statement in materials.

### Personal-attribute avoidance on paid ads
- ✅ **Do:** "When traditional pills fall short, Rugiet Ready could be a good option to try."
- ❌ **Don't:** "You have ED — try Rugiet Ready."

### Comparative and superiority claims
- ✅ **Permitted:** naming active ingredients (sildenafil, tadalafil) factually.
- ❌ **Prohibited:** naming competitors (e.g., Viagra, Cialis) or saying Rugiet products definitively work "better" or are "safer" than other products without head-to-head data.

### Speed and duration claims
- Use qualifiers: "about 15 minutes on average," "up to 36 hours."
- Always pair with a disclaimer: "Individual results may vary."

### Testimonials and social proof
- Use only real customer feedback.
- Disclose any incentives.
- For public reviews, state that customer results are not independently verified.

## 6. Channel-specific call-outs

All marketing channels require disclaimers. Key channel-specific details:

| Channel | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Podcast reads** | End-of-spot disclaimers are only acceptable if read clearly and at normal pace. |
| **Meta / Instagram ads** | Avoid 2nd-person medical references (e.g., "you have ED"). Avoid the word "sex" unless used in an educational context. Alluding to pleasure will risk rejection. |
| **Google / YouTube ads** | Landing pages must include cost of medication. |
| **TikTok** | More stringent on scenes of intimacy than other paid social platforms. |
